London Travel Tips

Make the best of your trip by following these easy travelling tips. Useful information
for London visitors such as public transport and travelcards.

1. Plan your journey

Our online journey planner will help you find the best route for your journey around London and will tell you how long it will take. If you have a smartphone or any other location/aware device, you can plan your journey on the move.

Plan your journey at
tfl.gov.uk/journeyplanner

2. Travel off peak

The best times to travel are Mondays to Fridays between 06:30- 09:30 and 16:00 – 19:00. Travel outside these times for a more comfortable and less busy journey. If you use a Visitor Oyster card, an Oyster card or a UK – issued contactless payment card to pay as you go, your fares will also be cheaper if you travel off- peak.

3. Check before you travel

Check before you travel to avoid any service delays or disruptions and continue your journey. Visit tfl.gov.uk/check or ask a member of staff.

For updates and travel advice about major works and events that could affect your journey, visit tfl.gov.uk/major-works-and-events

4. Enjoy the city on foot

Walking in London is a great way to get around – it could be quicker than you think and its free ! Look out for our blue and yellow street signs and maps – they give you useful information about the local area including key landmarks, with realistic five and fifteen walking times.

5. Touch your card in the readers

On tube, DLR, London Overground, TFL Rail and National Rail services: To pay the right fare, always touch your oyster card, Visitor Oyster card or contactless payments card on the yellow card readers at the gates when you start and when you finish your journey. If you don’t do this, you might be charged a maximum fare. On buses and trams, you only need to touch your card on the card reader at the start of your journey.

6. Transport accessibility

If you have special transport needs, or if you are travelling with luggage or a pushchair there are a wide range of accessible transport options so you can travel more easily around London.

Visit tfl.gov.uk/transport-accessibility to download accessibility guides and maps, like the step free stations map for wheelchair users or call +44 (0) 343 222 1234* to find out more.
